# About

Ethan Jahn is a computational astrophysicist and consultant for the High Performance Computing systems at the University of Arizona, where he supports researchers and their workflows across academic disciplines. His background as an Adjunct Professor of Physics informs his current role leading HPC workshops, which range from foundational topics like Intro to HPC, Linux, and data management to specialized areas such as parallel computing and machine learning. His work as a digital artist has led to cross-disciplinary collaborations, and he continues to explore the connections between computing technology and research across fields, working to translate that knowledge for researchers at all levels.
